Erin Davisson Biography – Erin Davisson Wiki

Erin Davisson is a Television Anchor and Reporter at WFVR-TV, Local 5 News in Green Bay, Wisconsin, United States. Davisson joined WFRV-TV in March 1988 and has been at the Local 5 News for more than three decades. Born in Beloit, Wisconsin, United States, Davisson began her Television news career at Channel 7 in Wausau where she served as an Anchor and Reporter. She also worked in radio as a reporter and announced in both commercial and public radio.

Since joining WFRV-TV in Green Bay, Wisconsin, she has earned numerous reporting and anchoring awards and has been voted Best Anchor in newspaper polls in the Appleton Post Crescent and the Green Bay Press-Gazette. Some of her iconic Television career highlights include appearing on the Oprah Winfrey Show for a segment on television anchorwomen, anchoring live coverage of the Green Bay Packers Super Bowl wins in New Orleans and Dallas, and moderating a speaker series at the Weidner Center that let her rub elbows with such notables as Madeline Albright, Joan Rivers, Anna Quindlen, and Maya Angelou.

Erin is a graduate of the University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point, where she studied English. She has been presented with the school’s Communications Alumni of the Year Award. During her time at the University of Wisconsin-Stevens Point, Erin worked at 90FM, the student radio station as host and co-host of an alternative music radio show called ‘WaveWerks’, wrote for the UW-SP Alumni Newspaper, and for the student newspaper.
She also attended the Northeast Wisconsin Technical College in Niagara, Wisconsin, United States. When she is not on her reporting tasks, Davisson enjoys Traveling, listening to podcasts, kayaking, and enjoying the Northwoods.
