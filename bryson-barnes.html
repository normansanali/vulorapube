Bryson Barnes Bio, Age, Height, Parents, Wife, Salary, Wiki

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, August 9, 2024

Bryson Barnes is a young professional American football player. He is known to currently play football for his team Utah Utes where he plays the role of a quarterback. He has been into playing football since a very young age and is known to have won many awards for his amazing playing skills and techniques.

Early Life

Bryson is a young man whose exact date of birth or the year of birth is not known. He is the son of Chris Barnes, his father, and his mother Stacy Barnes whose professions are not known. Bryson was born in Milford in Southern Utah, where he grew up raising almost 12000 pigs.

He spent his childhood with his three brothers and a sister. Barnes attended Milford High School from where he graduated and later enrolled at the University of Utah, where he is currently studying. 

Bryson Barnes Career

Bryson started his football career when he committed to play football for his high school, where he played from 2016 to 2019. During his high school career, he won two state championships after which he received scholarships from FCS schools SUU and Western Illinois but he decided to go to the University of Utah.

In ten games, including one start, Bryson Barnes completed 37 of 57 passes, with 2 interceptions and 4 touchdowns, accumulating 430 yards. He also rushed 20 times for 116 yards. Bryson had a breakthrough moment when he earned his first-ever start against Washington State.

During this game, he achieved personal bests with 17 completions out of 27 attempts, totaling 175 passing yards, including a 31-yard pass to Money Parks. On the ground, he rushed 8 times for 51 yards. In the Rose Bowl matchup against Penn State, Bryson Barnes managed 10 completions on 19 passes for 112 yards, including 1 interception and 1 touchdown.

Another standout performance came against Southern Utah, where he was perfect, going 6-for-6 on passes for 98 yards and a touchdown. Facing Colorado, Bryson Barnes displayed versatility by completing 4 of 5 passes for 45 yards and securing another touchdown.

His rushing game reached its peak during this game, gaining a career-high 62 yards on the ground, highlighted by a 32-yard carry. Acknowledging his dedication to academics, Bryson Barnes was named to the Pac-12 Academic Honor Roll.

In 2021, Bryson Barnes made appearances in three games: Weber State, Colorado, and against Ohio State. During the Rose Bowl clash with Ohio State, he entered the game with less than 10 minutes remaining and completed 2 passes out of 2 attempts for 23 yards, culminating in a 15-yard touchdown pass.
