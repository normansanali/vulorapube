Early Life and Career Beginnings

Brooke Williamson was born and raised in Los Angeles, California. She developed a passion for cooking at a young age and pursued her culinary dreams by attending the Los Angeles Culinary Institute. After graduation, she worked in various renowned restaurants, honing her skills and gaining valuable experience.

Rise to Fame

Williamson’s big break came when she was cast in the fourteenth season of Top Chef, where she emerged as the winner. This victory significantly boosted her career and public recognition, leading to numerous opportunities in the culinary world.

Restaurant Ventures

Brooke Williamson, along with her husband Nick Roberts, owns and operates several successful restaurants in Los Angeles. These include Hudson House, The Tripel, Playa Provisions, and Da Kikokiko. Public Speaking Engagements

Online Cooking Classes
