Jake Paul Net Worth How Much Is Jake Paul Worth

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, August 28, 2024

Jake Paul Net Worth – Jake Paul is an American social media personality and professional boxer who was best known for his work on Vine but played Dark Man for two seasons on the Disney Channel series Bizardvark.

Jake Paul’s actions have been the subject of much controversy throughout his career, including charges of trespassing and illegal assembly. Jake Paul’s boxing career began in August 2018 when he defeated British YouTuber Desi Oratunzi by TKO in the amateur tournament in the fifth round.

Jake Paul turned pro and defeated YouTuber AnEsonGib in January 2020 by TKO in the first round. From 2020 to 2022, Jake Paul won a second-round KO against former basketball player Nate Robinson in subsequent bouts, and retired mixed martial artists Ben Askren, Tyrone Woodley, and Anderson Silva. In January 2023, he announced that he would be making his professional MMA debut with the PFL.

What is Jake Paul Net Worth?

In January 2022, Forbes announced that Jake Paul made approximately $38 million from boxing in 2021, making him the 46th highest-paid athlete in the world for that period and he’s reported to be worth between $17–30 million. According to Celebrity Net Worth, he is worth $60 million.

Jake Paul Early Life

Jake Paul was born in Cleveland, Ohio, and grew up in Westlake, Ohio, with his older brother Logan, who is also a YouTuber and internet personality. They started filming themselves when Jake was 10. Their parents are Pamela Ann Stepnick and realtor Gregory Allan Paul.

Jake Paul started his career in September 2013 by posting videos on Vine. When Vine was discontinued by Twitter Inc., Jake Paul said he had 5.3 million followers and 2 billion app views. Jake Paul opened a YouTube channel on May 15, 2014. His channel is known for pranks, controversy, and hip-hop music.

After critical acclaim on Vine and YouTube, Jake Paul appeared on the set of the Disney Channel series Bizarre Vark, where he played a character who accepted bold demands and was set to play it. On July 22, 2017, in the middle of filming Season 2 of Bizardvark, Disney Channel announced that Jake Paul was stepping down from the series.

The announcement follows reports by KTLA that residents in Paul’s neighborhood have publicly complained about the noise caused by Paul’s pranks, parties, fire hazards, and large numbers of his fans gathering in the neighborhood.

Jake Paul later confirmed the news on his Twitter page, saying he would now focus more on his personal brand, his YouTube channel, his business ventures, and more adult acting roles. Jake Paul later revealed in an interview with The Hollywood Reporter that he was actually fired from Bizaardvark by Disney, which wanted to expedite the process of weaning him off the show due to the KTLA segment.

Jake Paul Boxing career And Earnings

On August 25, 2018, Jake Paul and his brother Logan Paul faced KSI and his brother Desi Oratunzi in two amateur boxing matches. Jake Paul’s match against Desi was the most important undercard before the main event, KSI vs. Logan Paul. Jake Paul defeated Desi by technical knockout in the fifth round.

On December 21, 2019, it was announced that Jake Paul would make his professional boxing debut on January 30, 2020, in Miami against YouTuber AnEsonGib (a.k.a. Gib). The fight between Jake Paul and Gibb was also featured in the WBO world middleweight title fight between professional boxers Demetrius Andrade and Luke Keeler. Jake Paul won the bout with two TKOs.
He finished 18th in the first round.

In July 2020, it was announced that Jake Paul would be venturing into the ring for a second professional bout, facing professional basketball player Nate Robinson as part of the undercard for the Mike Tyson vs. Roy Jones Jr. exhibition match.

The event was initially scheduled for September 12 at the Dignity Health Sports Park in Carson, California; however, in August, Tyson revealed the event had been pushed back to November 28 to maximize revenue. Jake Paul won the fight via KO at 1:24 in the second round.

On September 6, 2022, it was announced that Jake Paul’s fight with former UFC champion Anderson Silva would take place on October 29 at £187 for eight rounds of three minutes. Jake Paul defeated Silva by unanimous decision 78-73 (twice), 77-74. On 27 January 2023, it was announced that Jake Paul would face Tommy Fury on 26 February in Saudi Arabia. On match night, Jake Paul lost by split decision despite taking Fury down in eight rounds. One judge scored Paul 75-74 and the other two judges scored Fury 76-73.

Jake Paul walked away from the fight against Deji earning around £2.4m ($3m); he earned over £3.3m in 2020 following his fights with AnEsonGib and Nate Robinson. In 2021, it was reported that Jake Paul earned around £33m ($40m) after he fought three times that year – defeating Ben Askren and Tyron Woodley twice.

Jake Paul fought against former UFC legend Anderson Silva in October of 2022, and it was reported that he earned in the region of £5.4m ($6.5m) for the fight. In total, it is estimated that he has earned around £44m ($54m) inside the boxing ring.

Jake Paul Personal Life

Jake Paul is of English, Irish, Scottish, Welsh, Jewish, French, and German descent. His net worth is approximately $17 million to $30 million. In January 2022, Forbes announced that Jake Paul had earned approximately $38 million in boxing in 2021, making him the 46th highest-paid athlete in the world during the same period.

Jake Paul began dating fellow American YouTuber and internet personality Tana Mongeau in April 2019. In June 2019, the couple announced that they were engaged, although many fans and commentators did not believe that the engagement was legitimate. On July 28 of that year, Jake Paul and Mongeau exchanged vows in Las Vegas.

InTouch later reported that the couple had not obtained a marriage license prior to the ceremony and that the officiant was also not licensed by the state of Nevada. As a result, the marriage was not legally binding. Buzzfeed reported that Jake Paul and Mongeau left the ceremony separately. The ceremony, which was available on pay-per-view for $50, was recorded by MTV for the show No Filter: Tana Mongeau.

On an episode of the show, Mongeau stated that the ceremony was something “fun and lighthearted that we’re obviously doing for fun and for content.” The couple announced their break-up in January 2020. On April 3, 2023, Jake Paul and Dutch speedskater Jutta Lilledam publicly confirmed that they are together romantically after dating on Instagram a few months ago.
