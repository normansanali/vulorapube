Get To Know Lisa Loring Spouse, Children, Wednesday Addams

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Saturday, July 6, 2024

Veteran actress Lisa Loring has died, so her fans have been asking did she leave behind a husband. Does she have any children? Read all that we’ve discovered in this article below.

Tag along and learn more as you read on.

Lisa Loring Cause Of Death

Actress Lisa Loring passed away at the age of 64. The news of her death was confirmed by her daughter Vanessa Foumberg to Variety. She told the news outlet that the actress died on 28 January 2023. She reportedly suffered a stroke which became her cause of death. Moreover, she shared that the star “went peacefully with both her daughters holding her hands.”

According to a statement provided to CBS News by Loring’s rep, she was “a very loving mother, grandmother, and friend with a lifetime of amazing stories and experiences.” “She brought to life one of the most iconic characters in Hollywood history that is still celebrated today,” her agent, Chris Carbaugh, continued. “Lisa loved sharing her memories and meeting all her fans across the world. She will be missed dearly.”

Friends of the late celebrity also honored her legacy soon after her departure. Loring “is embedded in the tapestry that is pop culture and in our hearts always as Wednesday Addams,” a friend named Laurie Jacobson wrote on Facebook.

Furthermore, The Munsters actor Butch Patrick also shared a few words, writing in a Facebook post. They wrote, “Very sorry to hear of my dear friend Lisa Loring’s passing. We were very close and worked together often. I know she was very weak. I was in her company just a few weeks ago. Godspeed my friend.”

Did Lisa Loring Leave Behind A Spouse?

Lisa Loring married four times in her life. She married Farrell Foumberg, her childhood sweetheart, in 1973 when she was 15 years old, according to The Hollywood Reporter. The next year witnessed the birth of their daughter and the couple’s separation.

Doug Stevenson, an actor who had roles in movies including “The Prowler,” “Grave Secrets: The Legacy of Hilltop Drive,” and “Breaker! Breaker!” was the next person she married. Marianne Stevenson was born to the couple, but after two years of marriage, Lisa and Doug divorced.

She met adult movie actor Jerry Butler on the filming of the movie “Traci’s Big Trick” and they were married in 1987, according to The Guardian. She wed Jerry after he promised to stop watching porn. He nevertheless persisted in making covert appearances in adult movies, which led to a divorce in 1992. According to People, Lisa’s drug misuse issues started during her marriage to Jerry.

A third marriage she calls “the biggest mistake of my life”: her husband was acting in pornography and she didn’t approve of it. “I would not be involved with someone who did that … he was going behind my back and lying to me: that was it.”

In 2003, Lisa wedded Graham Rich. According to The Things, the pair split up in 2008 and filed for divorce in June 2014 after fourteen years of marriage. Divorce papers were filed at a court in Los Angeles, citing irreconcilable differences, while Loring also asked for her estranged husband not to be granted spousal support.

With the exception of her third ex-husband, Lisa claimed that she still gets along well with all of her former husbands.

Lisa Loring Children

Lisa Loring was a mother of two daughters Vanessa Foumberg and Marianne Stevenson. Vanessa was born to Lisa and Farrell in 1974. The actress welcomed Marianne in 1984 with her ex-husband Doug Stevenson.

She was also a grandmother of two grandchildren Emiliana and Charles.

Lisa Loring Movies And TV Shows

Lisa Loring is best known for playing Wednesday Addams on the 1964-1966 sitcom The Addams Family from 6 to 8 years old. At the age of 3, Lisa had a career in modeling. Soon after, she started acting in movies as a child artist.

The Charles Addams comic strip “The Addams Family” (1938–1988), which had a wealthy aristocratic family who delighted in the macabre, was adapted for the first time to the screen in the sitcom.

Wednesday was the family’s always pessimistic daughter in the comic strip. The character as portrayed by Loring was amiable but odd. This Wednesday had a collection of dolls with severed heads, and she had called the queen of France who had been executed, Marie Antoinette, after her favorite doll. As a hobby, Wednesday raised spiders.

Loring began working as a child model in 1961. She eventually started acting, and according to rumors, she appeared in a 1964 episode of the medical drama “Dr. Kildare” as a guest star. Loring was only six years old when she was chosen to play Wednesday in “The Addams Family.” Her first regular television role, it was. The two seasons and 64 episodes of the show concluded in 1966.

Other TV shows and movies are The Phyllis Diller Show, The Girl from U.N.C.L.E., Halloween with the New Addams Family, Fantasy Island, Gabe and Walker, Bloody Frenzy, Savage Harbor Way Down in Chinatown, and more.
