Leila Janah - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, Career, Married, Facts

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, September 8, 2024

Table of Biography

Leila Janah was a businesswoman from Lewiston, New York, the United States. Leila Janah was the Founder and CEO of Samasource and LXMI which share a common social mission to end global poverty by giving work to people in need.

Early Life

Leila Janah was born near Niagara Falls in Lewiston, New York, the United States on October 9, 1982. Leila’s zodiac sign was Libra and she was 37 years old at the time of her death.

Moreover, Leila grew up in San Pedro, Los Angeles, California after her parents moved there. Leila was the daughter of Indian immigrants who came to the United States with nothing.

Her father’s name is Sahadev Chirayath and her mother’s name is Martine Janah. Furthermore, Leila’s father worked as a structural engineer and her mother worked as a worker.

Leila described her childhood as difficult because of the lack of financial security. Moreover, Leila has a brother named Ved Janah.

Leila Janah studied and attended the California Academy of Mathematics and Science in Carson, California. Moreover, Leila won a scholarship through American Field Services at the age of 17 and convinced them to let her spend it teaching in Ghana.

Furthermore, Leila spent 6 months in Ghana during her senior year of high school. Later, Leila joined and graduated with a degree in African Development Studies from Harvard University in 2005.

Death

On January 24, 2020, Leila Janah died from epithelioid sarcoma a rare form of cancer. During her death, Leila was 37 years old.

Leila Janah – Net Worth 2024

Leila garnered a significant amount of money as the Founder and CEO of Samasource and LXMI. The estimated net worth of Leila Janah was around $500 thousand and $800 thousand US dollars.

Achievements

Leila Janah was a Young Global Leader of the World Economic Forum, a 2012 TechFellow, a Director of CARE USA, and a Club de Madrid Young Leadership Award.

In 2014, Leila became the youngest person to win a Heinz Award when she received the 19th Annual Heinz Award in Technology, the Economy, and Employment.

In 2016, Elle Magazine included Leila in “Women in Tech”. Forbes named Leila a “Rising Star” on Forbes’s 30 Under 30 list.

Professional Life

Leila Janah has worked several jobs like babysitting and tutoring since her teenage. Leila began her career as a management consultant with Katzenbach Partners, a company that specializes in healthcare, outsourcing, and mobile.

Moreover, Leila’s first assignment with Katzenbach was managing a call center in Mumbai, India. Due to her experience from the assignment, Leila sparked the inspiration for Samasource which is the non-profit organization that she founded in 2008.

Leila established Samahope in 2012, which was the first crowdfunding platform to directly pay doctors who deliver life-changing medical treatments to women and children in poor communities.

Furthermore, Samahope directly funds doctors’ Life-changing medical treatments for women and children in need.

Leila’s organization Samahope was built on the belief that transparent funding mechanisms could help close the global surgery gap. Moreover, Samahope also ensures that all people have access to medical treatments.

Leila also founded Samaschool in 2013 which is a program that moves people out of poverty by providing digital skills training. Moreover, Samaschool also provides a connection to internet-based jobs that pay a living wage for needy people.

Furthermore, Samaschool runs in-person programs in Arkansas, California, New York, and Kenya. Additionally, Samaschool also provides online classes that are available internationally.

Moreover, Samaachool courses train students in digital literacy, workforce, portfolio building, and readiness. Leila also co-founded LXMI in 2015 which is a for-profit luxury skincare brand. Moreover, LXMI is built on the idea of beauty in action.

LXMI also engages marginalized women in remote Nile Valley villages to harvest LXMI ingredients, with their producers earning three times the local average salary. LXMI is also called after the Hindu goddess of beauty and prosperity, Lakshmi.
