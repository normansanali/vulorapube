Christian Gálvez Wife: Meet Patricia Pardo

Is Christian Gálvez married? Yes, Christian Galvez is married to Patricia Pardo.

Christian was previously married to Almudena Cid in Torrelodones, Madrid in 2010 after t hey dated for three years. Almudena is a former Spanish individual rhythmic gymnast who competed on the Spanish national team. They divorced in 2022.

Gálvez embarked on his career in the world of entertainment at a young age, making his acting debut at 15 in the popular Spanish TV series “Médico de familia.” His talent soon led him to prominent roles in television shows like “La casa de los líos” (1996) and “Al salir de clase” (1998).

Between 1999 and 2002, Gálvez expanded his career by hosting television programs such as “Verano noche,” “Humor a toda marcha,” and “Desperado Club Social.” His charismatic presence and wit made him a beloved figure on Spanish television.

In addition to his television work, Christian Gálvez also lent his voice to the video game “Little Big Planet,” showcasing his versatility as a performer.

One of his notable roles was as a reporter on the popular show “Caiga quien caiga” (2005–2007), aired on Telecinco and hosted by Manel Fuentes.

In 2007, he took over as the host of the game show “Pasapalabra,” a role he held for an impressive eleven years. During his tenure, the show achieved the highest ratings in Spain and received prestigious awards such as the Premios Ondas (2008) and several TP de Oro awards.

While hosting “Pasapalabra,” Gálvez also found time to participate in other programs like the reality show “Operación Tony Manero” (2008), “Tú sí que vales” (2008–2013), “Supervivientes” (2009–2011), “El Reencuentro” (2011), and “Acorralados” (2011).

In 2010, he penned his book “Sin-vergüenzas por el mundo,” offering insights into his career as a reporter.

Christian Gálvez’s talent extended to the big screen, where he starred in the film “Ni pies ni cabeza” (2011), directed by Antonio del Real and featuring Jaydy Michel and Blanca Jara.

He also lent his voice to Napoléon Bonaparte in the video game “Assassin’s Creed: Unity” (2014).

In 2014, he published “Matar a Leonardo da Vinci,” showcasing his interest in history and art.

His passion for history led to him being named a global expert on Leonardo da Vinci in 2017, with plans to exhume da Vinci’s remains, recover his DNA, and reconstruct the Renaissance genius’s face.

Gálvez’s career continued to flourish as he presented the talent show “¡Vaya fauna!” (2015), featuring animals as stars.

Despite legal issues related to the program’s portrayal of animals, Gálvez stood firm in his support for the show.

In 2016, he published his second novel, “Reza por Miguel Ángel.”

His fascination with history persisted, leading to the publication of “Leonardo da Vinci: cara a cara” in 2017 and “Gioconda descodificada” in 2018, focusing on the role of women in Renaissance art.

From 2021 to 2022, Gálvez hosted the radio show “De sábado con Christian Gálvez” on Cadena 100 and later took on hosting duties for “Alta tensión,” the Spanish adaptation of the American game show “Wipeout.”

Following the show’s cancellation, he was announced as the host of “25 palabras,” the Spanish adaptation of the American game show “25 Words or Less.”

In 2010, Gálvez was honored as the herald of La Línea de la Concepción and received the honorary card for the Real Balompédica Linense.

Gálvez met Almudena Cid in the course of hosting “Pasapalabra,” and the two dated for three years before tying the knot on August 7, 2010, in Torrelodones, Madrid. However, they eventually divorced in 2022. Galvez is currently married to Patricia Prado.

Throughout his dynamic career, Christian Gálvez has consistently proven himself as a multifaceted talent, captivating audiences with his charisma and versatility in the world of entertainment and literature.
