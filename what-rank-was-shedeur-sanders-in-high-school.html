What rank was Shedeur Sanders in high school?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, August 26, 2024

Shedeur Sanders was ranked as the No. 37 quarterback in his high school football class and the No. 75 player from Texas. Despite being a three-star prospect with an 88 rating on 247 Sports, Sanders showcased exceptional skills and potential on the field. He had an impressive high school career, winning state championships and displaying his abilities as a dual-threat quarterback. With an athletic frame and the ability to extend plays, Sanders demonstrated poise and calmness in the pocket, making him a standout player at the high school level.

Sanders comes from a football-oriented family, with his father, Deion Sanders, being a legendary NFL player and his brother, Shilo Sanders, playing college football as a cornerback. This family connection likely played a role in shaping Shedeur's passion for the sport and his determination to excel at the highest level.

Despite his solid tools and promising skills, Sanders initially committed to play college football at Florida Atlantic. However, his transfer journey began when he decided to leave Jackson State University, where he was named the SWAC Freshman of the Year and won the Jerry Rice Award as the most outstanding freshman in the FCS. In 2022, Sanders transferred to the University of Colorado Boulder, bringing his talents to the Power 5 level.

In addition to his on-field success, Sanders has also garnered attention off the field. He has signed NIL deals with prominent brands such as Gatorade, Beats by Dre, and BRADY. These opportunities highlight the transformative power of NIL agreements in college sports, allowing athletes like Sanders to capitalize on their talents and create a brand for themselves.

While Shedeur Sanders' high school ranking may not have been as high as some other football prospects, it is evident that he possesses the potential to make a significant impact at the college level and potentially even reach the NFL. His skills, combined with his family background and the opportunities presented by NIL agreements, make him a player to watch as he continues to develop and make a name for himself in the world of football.

In conclusion, Shedeur Sanders was ranked as the No. 37 quarterback in his high school football class and the No. 75 player from Texas. Despite these rankings, Sanders has proven himself to be a highly productive and talented quarterback, winning state championships and displaying dual-threat abilities. His family connection to football legends and his signing of NIL deals further emphasize his potential for success. As Sanders continues his football journey at the University of Colorado Boulder, it will be exciting to see how he further develops and makes his mark on the sport.
